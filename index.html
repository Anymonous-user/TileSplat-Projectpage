TileSplat: Practical System for Real-Time Streaming and Browser Rendering of City-Scale Photorealistic 3D Scenes

Anynomous Author
Paper Code(Comming Soon) Demo

Fast loading demo: the viewer displays an instant coarse view and quickly sharpens details as tiles arrive. Adaptive streaming keeps latency low and interaction fluid, even under changing bandwidth.

Abstract

There is a growing demand for high-fidelity, interactive 3D web content, spurred by its adoption in fields like digital twins, cul- tural heritage, and e-commerce. However, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS), the state-of-the-art technique for generating such photore- alistic scenes, typically produces monolithic, multi-gigabyte files that are fundamentally incompatible with the progressive loading and resource limits of standard web browsers. To bridge this gap, we present TileSplat, an end-to-end system to enable the seamless streaming and real-time rendering of city-scale scenes on the web. Our contributions are threefold. First, TileSplat novelly splits massive 3DGS data into a spatial tile hierarchy with quality-preserving levels of detail (LOD), maintaining high visual fidelity across view distances. Second, we propose adaptive streaming protocol combin- ing real-time trajectory prediction with adaptive quality selection under varying network conditions. Third, our WebGL-optimized rendering pipeline with hierarchical cache management achieves 30+ FPS rendering on city-scale scenes. Extensive evaluation demon- strates TileSplat uniquely renders scenes that cause competing systems to fail (out-of-memory), achieving superior visual qual- ity with 2.2× bandwidth reduction versus commercial progressive streaming and 4.3× versus full-scene loading. Trajectory prediction improves cache hit rates from 68.3% to 91.2%, reducing time-to- good-quality by 42%. These results validate that tile-based stream- ing with predictive prefetching successfully deploys photorealistic 3D content within browser constraints.
